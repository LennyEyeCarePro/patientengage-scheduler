FAQ

Questions Practice Owners Ask

Every question below is one we hear regularly. Here is the honest answer to each one.

"Won't Google own my patient data?" ↓
No. Google Reserve is a button that directs patients to YOUR booking page. Google does not receive or store the patient's appointment details, health information, or contact data. The patient clicks through to your system, and from that point forward, everything happens on your terms. This is fundamentally different from ZocDoc, where the patient books within ZocDoc's system and is treated as a ZocDoc customer.
"Could someone activate this on my listing without my consent?" ↓
Not through PatientEngage. Google Reserve is a toggle that only you control within your PatientEngage dashboard. No third party can activate it on your behalf. This addresses a real concern in the industry: some practices have reported that platforms like ZocDoc have activated booking on their Google listings without them realizing they gave consent. With PatientEngage, nothing happens until you flip the switch.
"Will this increase my no-show rate?" ↓
Google Reserve links patients to the same booking flow they would use on your website. They still complete your full intake process, select a time, and confirm. The only difference is how they got there: one click instead of three or four. Patients who actively search for and book an appointment have high intent, making impulsive no-shows unlikely.
"I already have online booking on my website." ↓
Google Reserve is an additional booking channel, not a replacement. It catches patients BEFORE they even visit your website. Data shows that for some practices, Google Reserve clicks match or exceed website booking clicks. It is additive to your existing system.
"How hard is the setup?" ↓
One toggle. That is it. Navigate to Google Reserve in your PatientEngage dashboard, flip it to On, and choose where the button links. It syncs with Google automatically and goes live within 24 hours. No code. No IT department. No technical work.
