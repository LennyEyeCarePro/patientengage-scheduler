# Step 6: Pre-Gen Q&A — Campaigns-To-Go

## Confirmed Answers

### 1. What is the primary angle for this page?
**Answer:** "Your patients need to hear from you — now they will." Position Campaigns-To-Go as the solution to the #1 marketing barrier: the blank canvas. Practices know they should market but can't execute. Lead with the problem (empty content calendars, silent social feeds, sporadic emails that stop after 2 months) and solve it with a done-for-you campaign library that pushes across 5 channels in one click. The core message: professional marketing without a marketing department.

### 2. What is the killer stat?
**Answer:** 3,728,419 emails sent in a single month across 647 practices — proving the infrastructure works at scale. For ROI: 34,623 EHR email appointments attributed to campaigns in March 2026 — direct proof that campaign emails drive patients into chairs. For case study: one practice sent 132,220 emails from a single Dry Eye Rescue campaign → 42,250 unique opens (32%) → 1,468 appointments booked → $514K in campaign-influenced revenue from one send.

### 3. Who is the target persona?
**Answer:** Primary: Practice owner (OD) who knows they should be marketing but doesn't have the time, expertise, or staff to execute consistently. They've tried Mailchimp, posted to Facebook a few times, and given up. Secondary: Office manager who's been told to "do more marketing" but has zero content creation skills and no budget for an agency. Tertiary: Multi-location group operator who needs consistent branding across all offices without hiring per-location marketing coordinators.

### 4. What is the feature name as patients/practices would say it?
**Answer:** "Campaigns" or "marketing campaigns" or "email campaigns." The internal name is "Campaigns-To-Go." Practices see a calendar with "Live Campaigns" and "Future Campaigns." The EyeCarePro product page uses "Campaigns-To-Go" with the tagline "Marketing at the Touch of a Button."

### 5. What is the primary CTA?
**Answer:** "Launch Your First Campaign Today" — with secondary: "Already an EyeCarePro client? Ask your marketing manager about activating Campaigns-To-Go." The one-click simplicity should be echoed in the CTA language.

### 6. What are the true differentiators vs. competitors?
**Answer:**
1. **Done-for-you content library** — hundreds of pre-built, eye care-specific campaigns vs. blank canvas (Mailchimp) or generic templates (Solutionreach)
2. **5-channel coordination** — Email + FB + IG + GBP + Landing Page in one click. No other optometry platform does all 5.
3. **Eye care specificity** — Dry Eye, Allergies, UV Protection, Save Vision Month, brand partnerships (Marchon, VSP, Alcon) — not repurposed dental templates
4. **Appointment attribution** — EHR Email Appointments track campaign → booked exam. Real ROI, not theoretical.
5. **Managed service** — EyeCarePro's team handles scheduling and oversight for many practices. Not self-service-only.
6. **Scale proof** — 3.7M emails/month with <0.1% bounce and complaint rates. The infrastructure is battle-tested.

### 7. Who are the real competitors?
**Answer (ranked by competitive threat):**
1. **Solutionreach** — Legacy engagement platform. EHR-triggered campaigns. But template-based, email-only, no multi-channel content library. $300–$500/mo.
2. **Demandforce (Internet Brands)** — Bundled email + reviews + scheduling. But generic health templates, not eye care-specific. $300–$400/mo.
3. **Mailchimp / Constant Contact** — DIY email tools. Cheap ($20–$80/mo) but blank canvas = 90% of practices quit within 3 months.
4. **Weave / RevenueWell** — Bundled phone + text + some marketing. But marketing campaigns are an afterthought, not the core product.
5. **Marketing agencies** — The true alternative. $2,000–$5,000/month. Campaigns-To-Go delivers equivalent multi-channel execution at a fraction of the cost.

### 8. What are the top 3 benefits (ROI + operational)?
**Answer:**
1. **Replace the Content Calendar (Done-For-You)** — Pick a campaign, click schedule, done. No planning, designing, writing, or coordinating across platforms. One click replaces an entire month of marketing effort.
2. **Multi-Channel Without Multi-Platform (5 Channels, 1 Click)** — A single campaign fires across Email, Facebook, Instagram, GBP, and a Landing Page. This is what agencies charge $3K/month for.
3. **Appointment Attribution (Prove Marketing ROI)** — The dashboard connects campaign emails to booked exams. 34,623 appointments attributed in a single month. Not theoretical — measurable.

### 9. What objections will the FAQ need to handle?
**Answer (7 key objections):**
1. "I already use Mailchimp / Constant Contact" → Blank canvas problem. How many campaigns did you actually send?
2. "We already have Solutionreach / Demandforce" → They do reminders, not marketing campaigns. When was their last multi-channel Spring Allergies campaign?
3. "We don't have time for marketing" → That's the point. Everything is pre-built. One click. Your team doesn't create anything.
4. "Email marketing doesn't work anymore" → 21% open rates in healthcare. Plus it's not just email — 5 channels catches patients everywhere.
5. "We tried campaigns before and stopped" → Blank canvas problem again. This removes the wall. Content exists. Design exists. Schedule exists.
6. "How do I know it drives appointments?" → EHR Email Appointments tracking. 34,623 appointments in one month. Real attribution.
7. "My patients don't open marketing emails" → 21% is strong. But multi-channel means they see it on Facebook, Instagram, or GBP even if they skip the email.

### 10. What is the case study practice?
**Answer:** Maine Optometry → anonymized as "Coastal Eye Associates." Full-service optometry practice in New England with a large patient base. March 2026: single "Dry Eye Rescue" campaign → 132,220 emails sent, 42,250 unique opens (32% open rate), 1,150 email clicks (0.9%), 1,468 EHR email appointments (1.1%). $514K in campaign-influenced revenue from one send. Zero effort from the practice — they selected the campaign from the library, scheduled it, and the system handled everything.

### 11. What is the positioning strategy?
**Answer:** "Done-For-You Multi-Channel Marketing." Not competing with Mailchimp on email features or Solutionreach on automation. Competing on the premise that practices DON'T WANT to be marketers — they want marketing to happen FOR them. The library eliminates the blank canvas. The multi-channel execution eliminates the platform juggling. The managed service eliminates the follow-through problem. The appointment attribution eliminates the "does this work?" doubt.
