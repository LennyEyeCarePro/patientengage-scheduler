The problem

Three ways practices lose patients they have already seen.

Each one looks different. All three end the same way: a patient who would have come back simply drifts away.

59% voicemail
Your staff is calling into voicemail.
15-20 hours a month dialing overdue patients. Our data shows 59% of those calls go to voicemail. The ones who do answer are interrupted during their day. Patients respond to texts within minutes. They ignore calls from numbers they do not recognize.
Single touch
Your EHR sends one email and hopes for the best.
Built-in EHR reminders send a single email before an appointment. No recall for overdue patients. No SMS. No follow-up if nobody responds. One message through one channel does not move people to act. It takes multiple touches across text, email, and call to cut through.
Most common
Nobody notices when patients lapse.
20-30% of patients quietly become overdue every year. Not because they are unhappy. Because nobody reminded them. A 3,000-patient practice loses 600-900 patients annually. At $350 per comprehensive exam, that is $210,000-$315,000 walking out the door.

Common questions

Things practice owners ask before saying yes.

Every objection below is one we hear regularly. Here is the honest answer to each one.

"My patients are 65+. They do not do texting. They want a phone call from a human." ↓
This is the most common myth. Over 70% of seniors now prefer digital communication for routine healthcare tasks because they can read a text at their own pace without struggling to hear a muffled voicemail. And here is the real pivot: by automating the routine reminders for your younger patients, your staff actually has more time to give those high-touch 80-year-old patients the long phone calls they genuinely appreciate. The software handles the noise. Your team handles the nuance.
"We are a boutique, family-oriented practice. Automated texts make us look cold and corporate." ↓
Patients today — especially Millennials and Gen Z — actually view "calling to book" as a friction point, not a personal touch. To them, "personal" means "convenient." Think of it as a hybrid model: the software handles the routine reminders, while your staff handles the post-op follow-ups, complex insurance questions, and the moments that actually build relationships. Every message is personalized with the patient's name, their doctor, your practice name, and their appointment details. It does not feel corporate. It feels like your practice remembered them.
"I am already paying my front desk staff to be here. Why would I pay for software to do what they already do?" ↓
Here is the math. A manual recall call costs ~$16 when you factor in labor, benefits, overhead, and the 3-4 minutes of phone tag per patient. An automated text costs pennies. But the real question is: do you want your $25/hour office manager being a high-level biller and frame stylist, or do you want them to be a human auto-dialer? Our data shows staff spend 15-20 hours a month on recall calls. That is half a week every month spent calling into voicemail. The software frees your best people to do what they are actually good at.
"Patients already get too many notifications. They will just unsubscribe or get annoyed." ↓
Irrelevant or spammy messages cause notification fatigue. Useful messages — like a link to their digital intake form or a "your glasses are ready" text — are actually valued. Our opt-out rate is 1% across 37,862 messages. The system uses cadence control: it is not about sending more messages, it is about sending the right message at the right time. A text 3 weeks before a patient's annual exam due date is not spam. It is exactly what they need to hear.
"What if we get sued? I have heard texting patients is not HIPAA compliant." ↓
If your practice is texting patients from a personal iPhone, that is the HIPAA risk. PatientEngage provides the Business Associate Agreement (BAA) and encryption that your current manual texting lacks. Every message goes through a HIPAA-compliant platform with proper consent tracking, opt-out management, and audit trails. We are the safe room. The real compliance risk is continuing to do recalls with unencrypted email and personal phones.
"My EHR already sends appointment reminders. Why do I need this?" ↓
EHR reminders send a single email before an appointment. PatientEngage sends up to 6 touches across SMS, email, and call — starting 7 days before and ending 2 hours before. More importantly, EHR systems do not do recalls: the proactive re-engagement of patients who are overdue for their annual exam. That is where the real revenue recovery happens. 431 overdue patients rebooked in a single month across 23 practices. Your EHR cannot do that.
"How is this different from Solutionreach or Weave?" ↓
Three key differences. First, PatientEngage is built exclusively for optometry — we have recall types like Order Pickup and Pre-Appointment Information that generic healthcare platforms do not offer. Second, our multi-touch sequences go deeper: up to 6 touchpoints per recall type with intelligent channel alternation. Third, it is integrated with your PatientEngage platform — Scheduler, Google Reserve, Reviews — one dashboard, one login, one support team. Weave requires buying their entire phone system ($300-600/month). Solutionreach is a separate platform with separate training and a separate bill.
