Common questions

Everything you're wondering

"We already post to social media." ↓
That's a great start. A fair question to ask: when was your last post, and was it clinically relevant to what patients are dealing with this season? Social Stream publishes Seasonal Allergies in spring, UV Protection in summer, and Dry Eye awareness in winter. Automatically, every week. Think of it as your always-on baseline. Whatever your team posts on top of that becomes a bonus, not the whole strategy.
"My staff handles our Facebook." ↓
Many practices rely on a talented team member for this, and it usually works beautifully until it doesn't. When they're with a patient or on the phone, the posts pause. The hidden cost adds up too: at $22/hr, five hours a week in Canva is roughly $440/month in staff time. Social Stream doesn't replace your team's voice. It carries the professional baseline so their time can go back to patients, and your calendar never goes dark when things get busy.
"We already use Hootsuite / Buffer." ↓
Those are solid tools, but they're blank canvases. Every practice we talk to signs up with great intentions, schedules a few posts, then the calendar goes quiet. The tool assumes you already have a content strategy, design skills, and time. Social Stream flips that: we bring the content (professionally designed, eye-care specific, platform-optimized) and you just connect your accounts. Keep Hootsuite if you love it. Social Stream fills the gaps.
"Automated posts feel inauthentic." ↓
That concern usually comes from seeing bad automation. Generic "Happy Friday!" posts with stock photos. Social Stream looks nothing like that. It's professionally designed, clinically relevant content with separate imagery for each platform. Worth comparing: a consistent, polished presence versus a Facebook page that hasn't posted in six months. Patients notice both, and the quiet one tends to raise more eyebrows than the scheduled one.
"I don't think patients care about social media." ↓
Patients may not scroll looking for you, but Google pays close attention. Every post to your Google Business Profile signals that you're an active, engaged medical practice, and active profiles rank higher in the Map Pack. Quiet profiles get outranked by the ones posting weekly. Across our network, 214,509 social interactions happened in a single month, and the GBP posts quietly do the heavy lifting for local SEO.
"We use Weave / SolutionReach for this." ↓
Those platforms are excellent for appointment confirmations and recall texts. That's the administrative side. Social Stream covers the growth side: keeping your practice visible across Facebook, Instagram, and Google, and educating patients on services like Myopia Management or second pairs. They're complementary tools for different jobs. Weave is the phones. Social Stream is the storefront.
"We tried marketing before and it didn't work." ↓
That's a fair hesitation. Most bad experiences trace back to generic automated posts or generalist agencies with no eye care knowledge. Social Stream talks to the people who already know and trust you: your patients and their networks. Content is curated by a team that lives in optometry, so you're not getting a "see the world clearly" sunset post. Same quality of work a dedicated agency would produce, without the retainer.
"We'll do it later. We're too busy right now." ↓
We hear this often, and there's real logic to it. But Social Stream is specifically designed for the busy practice. Setup takes minutes (connect three accounts), and the content flows on its own after that. Every quiet week is a week patients can't tell if you're still open, and a week the larger competitors keep posting. The "right time" usually never arrives. The automation is what buys back the time.
