# Step 4 — Observe the Resulting View
## Feature: Social Posts (Social Stream)
**Date:** April 12, 2026
**Resulting View Location:** Practice's connected social channels (Facebook, Instagram, Google Business Profile)
**Practice Used for Observation:** North Park Vision (#1813, location 2274)

---

## Where the Patient-Facing Output Lives

Unlike Campaigns (which reach patients via email, landing pages, and social), Social Posts output lives **exclusively on the practice's social media channels**. There is no email, no landing page, no print component. The output appears in three places:

1. **Facebook Page** — posts published to the practice's Facebook business page
2. **Instagram Feed** — posts published to the practice's Instagram business account
3. **Google Business Profile** — posts published to the practice's GBP listing (visible in Google Maps and Google Search)

Patients encounter these posts organically — in their Facebook feed, on Instagram Explore, or when searching for the practice on Google. There is no direct outreach; Social Posts creates **ambient social presence**.

---

## What Patients See — By Channel [OBSERVED from Step 1]

### Facebook Post
- **What patients see:** A branded post in their Facebook feed from their eye doctor's practice page. The post includes a curated image (landscape format) with seasonal or educational content. Post copy is approximately 44 words, professionally written, with the practice's branding.
- **Content style:** Educational and seasonal — "Seasonal Allergies" tips, "Save Your Vision Month" awareness, "UV Protection" reminders, "New Season New Glasses" promotions
- **CTA:** Facebook posts include an optional CTA link pointing to the practice's website (configurable per-stream as "Practice Website")
- **Frequency:** Weekly for the main EyeCarePro stream (Wednesdays at 9 am EST), additional posts from specialty streams
- **Design quality:** Professional imagery, not stock-photo-feeling. Curated for eye care relevance.

### Instagram Post
- **What patients see:** A square-format image post with caption in their Instagram feed. Each post gets a separate image upload optimized for Instagram's square ratio (different from the landscape Facebook/Google image).
- **Content style:** Same educational/seasonal themes as Facebook, adapted visually for the Instagram format
- **CTA limitation:** Instagram doesn't support clickable links in post captions — the post caption serves awareness, not direct conversion
- **Frequency:** Same schedule as Facebook — posts go to both channels simultaneously

### Google Business Profile Post
- **What patients see:** A GBP post visible on the practice's Google Maps listing and in Google Search results for the practice name. Includes the post image and a CTA link.
- **Content style:** Same themes as Facebook/Instagram. GBP posts include an optional CTA link.
- **Visibility:** GBP posts appear in the "Updates" section of the practice's Google listing — visible to anyone searching for the practice or browsing Google Maps
- **SEO benefit:** Regular GBP posts signal activity to Google's local search algorithm, potentially boosting the practice's local SEO ranking

### What the Practice Doesn't Have to Do
This is the key observation: **the practice doesn't do anything.** They don't:
- Write post copy
- Select or upload images
- Schedule posting times
- Choose which platforms to post to
- Monitor posting success
- Create a content calendar

The calendar fills itself. Posts appear on Wednesdays (main stream) and throughout the week (specialty streams). The practice's only action is connecting their social accounts — after that, content flows automatically.

## Connection Warnings [OBSERVED]

When a social channel is NOT connected, the practice sees warnings:

- **Instagram:** "Warning: Instagram connection required to post for this location." + prominent "CONNECT INSTAGRAM" button
- **Facebook:** Similar warning if not connected
- **Google Business Profile:** Similar warning if not connected

These warnings are the primary "resulting view" friction point. The system has content ready to post, but it can't reach the channel until the practice connects it. From Step 2 data:
- 73% have Google connected — best adoption
- 45% have Facebook connected — moderate
- 20% have Instagram connected — massive gap

## Patient Experience Walkthrough

### The Organic Discovery Path
1. **Patient scrolls Facebook** → sees practice post about "Seasonal Allergies" in their feed → reads 44-word tip about eye allergy symptoms → associates practice with relevant health expertise
2. **Patient searches Google for practice name** → Google Business Profile shows recent "Save Your Vision Month" post in Updates section → practice looks active and engaged
3. **Patient browses Instagram** → sees practice's "UV Protection" post with professional imagery → follows the practice for more content

### The Ambient Brand Effect
Social Posts doesn't drive direct appointments the way Campaigns does. Instead, it creates **ambient brand presence** — patients see the practice regularly in their social feeds, which:
- Keeps the practice top-of-mind between visits
- Positions the practice as a professional, active business
- Creates touchpoints for patients who are passively considering an appointment
- Builds social proof (an active Facebook page with consistent posts looks more trustworthy than an abandoned one)

### Friction Points
- **Instagram caption links:** Instagram doesn't support clickable links in post captions. Patients who see the Instagram post can't click through to the practice website from the post itself. This is a platform limitation.
- **Google Business Profile visibility:** GBP posts are only visible to users who search for the practice specifically or browse Google Maps near the practice location. They don't appear in general social feeds.
- **No direct conversion tracking:** Unlike Campaigns (which track email → open → click → appointment), Social Posts has no direct conversion attribution. Interactions (impressions, likes, comments, shares) are tracked, but there's no "social post → appointment" tracking.

## Observations for Marketing Page

### The "Always-On" Story
The most compelling framing for Social Posts isn't "look at these amazing engagement numbers" — it's **"your social channels are never empty."** The value is consistency and professionalism over time.

**Recommended marketing framing:** "Your Facebook page hasn't been updated since 2023. Your Instagram has 3 posts. Your Google Business Profile says 'no recent updates.' Social Posts fixes all three — automatically, every week, with content designed for eye care."

### The Zero-Effort Promise
Social Posts is the purest "done for you" feature in PatientEngage. Campaigns require a practice to select and schedule a campaign. Reviews require patients to leave reviews. Social Posts requires nothing after the initial channel connection.

**Recommended marketing framing:** "Connect your social accounts once. Content starts flowing the same week. You'll never write a social media post again."

### The Scale Multiplier
One admin creates a post; 731 practices publish it. This is the leverage story.

**Recommended marketing framing:** "Our content team creates professional, seasonal eye care content. Your practice publishes it — automatically. Same effort as posting to one page, but it reaches 731 practices across the country."
