The problem

Three ways your front desk loses patients before they ever walk in.

Each one looks different. All three end the same way: a patient who was ready to book just went somewhere else.

Most common
Phone tag kills conversions.
Patients call, get put on hold, and hang up. Your team calls back, gets voicemail. Nobody wins. Your front desk spends 15 to 20 hours a month in voicemail loops, and the patients who never leave a message simply call the next practice on Google.
Website visitors
Your website visitors are leaving.
A patient visits your site, has a question about insurance or frames, but does not want to call. Without a text option, they bounce. That visitor was ready to book. They just needed a faster way to get one quick answer.
Automation fatigue
Automated texts feel impersonal.
Patients are drowning in automated appointment reminders and chatbot replies. They want to text a real person, not a robot. When every message from your practice feels like it was sent by a machine, trust erodes and patients stop engaging.

Common questions

Things practice owners ask before turning on texting.

Every question below is one we hear regularly. Here is the direct answer to each one.

Is 2-Way Texting HIPAA compliant? ↓
Yes. All conversations are fully encrypted and stored in a HIPAA-compliant dashboard with a signed BAA. The biggest HIPAA risk right now is likely staff using personal phones to text patients. Our dashboard is the secure alternative that keeps the practice out of legal trouble.
"I already have Weave or Solutionreach." ↓
Weave and Solutionreach are great for automated confirmations and recalls. But when a patient texts at 2:00 PM asking about Chrome Hearts frames, they do not want a bot. Our tool is the "Express Lane" for your staff to have real conversations that get lost in automated noise. It works alongside your existing tools, not instead of them.
"My staff is already buried. This sounds like more work." ↓
A phone call takes five minutes plus phone tag. A text takes thirty seconds. By giving patients a way to text, you are clearing phone lines. It is easier to manage five text conversations at once than two people on hold while a patient stands at the desk. Texting reduces the workload. It does not add to it.
"No EHR sync sounds like double work." ↓
For customer service questions, an EHR sync can actually be a liability. You do not need a clinical record of a patient asking about holiday hours or vision plans. Keeping this standalone means it is HIPAA-safe without sync-lag or software updates breaking your communication line. Think of it as a clean, fast hotline that works even if your EHR is down.
"My patients are 70+ and do not text." ↓
It is 2026. 75% or more of seniors prefer text for quick updates because they can read at their own pace without the pressure of a live phone conversation. Plus, moving quick questions to text frees your staff to give seniors the long, high-touch phone calls they actually want.
"What about after-hours texts?" ↓
An instant auto-reply captures the lead while protecting your team. Something like: "We are home with our families right now, but we have got your message and will jump on this first thing Monday morning." The patient knows they have been heard. Your staff does not have to respond at midnight.
How much does 2-Way Texting cost? ↓
2-Way Texting is included in all EyeCarePro marketing programs at no additional cost. Competitors charge $300 to $700 per month for bundled suites that include texting as one feature among many. We include the most important part, the actual conversation, as part of your marketing. No bundle tax.
Is this automated or does a real person respond? ↓
Every response comes from a real person on your team. This is not a chatbot or an AI auto-responder. It is human-to-human texting through a secure dashboard. That is the entire point. Patients want genuine answers from real staff, not canned replies. The only automated message is the optional after-hours auto-reply.
