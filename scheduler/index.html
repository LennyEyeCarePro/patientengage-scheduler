The problem

Three ways practices lose patients before they ever walk in.

Each one looks different on the surface. All three end the same way: a patient who was ready to book just booked somewhere else.

Phone only
The phone stops working after 5pm.
Every booking happens by phone during business hours. Calls at 7pm go to voicemail. Calls during patient exams go to voicemail. New patients who do not leave a message simply call the next practice on Google. The phone never shows you who you lost. The leakage is completely invisible.
Most common
A request form is not a booking.
The "Book Now" button on your website sends an email to the front desk. Not a booking, a lead. Staff call back. Phone tag. Manual EHR entry. No confirmation sent to the patient. The patient who submitted at 10pm is still waiting the next afternoon. By then, they have already booked somewhere else.
Both
Two channels, twice the manual work.
A phone line and a contact form, neither connected to the EHR. Patients use whichever they find first. Staff manage two inboxes and manually reconcile everything into the calendar. Double the channels, double the work, and the same problem: nothing is confirmed until a human intervenes on every single request.

"You did not lose that patient because of your care. You lost them because you were not there when they were ready."

Common questions

Things practice owners ask before saying yes.

Every objection below is one we hear regularly. Here is the honest answer to each one.

"I do not want patients dropping themselves into the wrong slots." ↓
Appointment types in the Real-Time Scheduler are mapped directly from your EHR. A patient booking a comprehensive exam can only land in a comprehensive exam slot. The EHR's own scheduling rules govern what is available and PatientEngage respects them entirely. Practices that want an extra layer of review can enable manual approval and confirm each request before it is finalized.
"I already have a Book Now button on my site." ↓
If your button sends an email to your front desk, you do not have online booking. You have a contact form. A contact form creates a lead that someone has to chase. The Real-Time Scheduler creates a confirmed patient who received an instant confirmation and is already in your EHR before anyone on your team sees the request. One captures patients. One loses them while you sleep.
"I already pay for Weave or SolutionReach." ↓
Weave is a phone system with scheduling features added. SolutionReach is a recall and retention platform. Neither was designed as a new patient acquisition channel. The Real-Time Scheduler works alongside your existing tools, not instead of them.
"My front desk is already overwhelmed." ↓
That is exactly why this exists. Every appointment the Real-Time Scheduler confirms automatically is one fewer callback, one fewer data entry task, and one fewer "did that get added to the schedule?" conversation. The queue shrinks. The phone stops ringing for bookings. Staff attention shifts to the patients already in the room.
"Our patients prefer to call. We do not want to push them online." ↓
Nobody is pushing your patients anywhere. The Real-Time Scheduler sits alongside your phone line and does not replace it. Patients who prefer to call still call. The Scheduler captures the ones who searched Google at 9pm, the ones who dislike being put on hold, and the ones who are new to the area and were not going to call a practice they have never heard of. Those patients were not going to call anyway. Without the Scheduler, you simply never hear from them.
