The problem

Your front desk spends 80% of its day on the phone. Calls still go unanswered.

Every practice has four windows where the phone goes unanswered. Most have accepted them as normal.

Dead Zone 1
The lunch hour
Many offices close from 12 to 1pm. Every call in that window goes to voicemail. Most patients do not leave a message. They call the next practice on Google.
Dead Zone 2
After hours
After 5pm, on weekends, and on holidays, every call goes to voicemail. Patients who search for an eye doctor at 8pm on a Sunday are ready to book. They will not wait until Monday morning for a callback.
Dead Zone 3
Busy periods
The front desk is already on a call. Another call rings through. It goes unanswered. This happens dozens of times per week at a typical practice. Staff cannot be in two conversations at once.
Dead Zone 4
Complex calls
A patient calls about picking up glasses and mentions their spouse needs an exam. A patient calls to ask about insurance before committing to a booking. These calls take 8 to 12 minutes and back up everyone waiting.

"Every missed call is a patient who found someone else. You just do not know their name."
