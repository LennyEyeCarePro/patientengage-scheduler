# Case Study: RingAnalytics — Southern Hills Eyecare

## Practice Profile
- **Anonymized Name:** Southern Hills Eyecare
- **Real Practice:** Parenti-Morris Eyecare - Rogers
- **Location:** Rogers, AR (single location)
- **Address:** 3710 West Southern Hills Boulevard, Rogers, AR 72758
- **Practice Type:** Full-service optometry (exams, glasses, contact lenses, insurance)
- **Data Period:** Q1 2026 (January 1 – March 30)

## The Challenge
Southern Hills Eyecare is a busy single-location practice in Rogers, Arkansas. With nearly 4,000 inbound calls per quarter, the office manager had no way to know which calls were converting to appointments, which marketing channels were driving the phone to ring, or why patients who called weren't ending up on the schedule. The phone was their most important patient touchpoint — and it was completely unmeasured.

## Revenue Math

### Attributable Revenue
- 590 booked appointment calls × $350 average comprehensive exam = **$206,500** in directly attributable phone revenue in Q1

### Recovery Opportunity
- 89 identified not-booked appointment calls × $350 = **$31,150** in recoverable revenue per quarter
- If the practice recovers even 50% of those: **$15,575 additional revenue per quarter** ($62,300/year)

### Full-Year Projection
- At current Q1 pace: ~2,360 booked calls/year = **$826,000** in annual phone-attributable revenue
- With 50% recovery of not-booked calls: **$888,300** annually

## What RingAnalytics Revealed

1. **The 87% capture rate is best-in-network** — but there's still $31K/quarter on the table from the 89 calls that didn't convert. RingAnalytics transcripts show the most common reasons: insurance confusion, scheduling conflicts, and patients asking about services the practice doesn't offer.

2. **Tuesday is the busiest call day** — but Friday afternoon is essentially dead. The practice could reallocate Friday afternoon phone staff to Tuesday coverage, or use Friday afternoon for callback follow-ups on missed/not-booked calls.

3. **741 hang-ups (19% of calls)** — These are calls where the patient gave up before connecting. Combined with 275 voicemails, that's over 1,000 potential touchpoints where the practice wasn't available. RingAnalytics makes this visible so the practice can adjust staffing.

4. **GMB drives the majority of appointment calls** — confirming that Google Business Profile optimization is the single most important marketing channel for this practice's phone revenue.
