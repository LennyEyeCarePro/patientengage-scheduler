The problem

Three ways practices lose patients before they ever walk in.

Each one looks different. All three have the same result: a patient who was ready to book just booked somewhere else.

Before state A
Phone only
Every booking happens by phone during business hours. Calls at 7pm go to voicemail. Calls during exams go to voicemail. New patients who don't leave a message simply call the next practice on Google. The phone never shows you who you lost — the leakage is invisible.
Before state B — most common
The form fallacy
The practice has a "Book Now" button that sends an email to the front desk. Not a booking — a lead. Staff call back. Phone tag. Manual EHR entry. No confirmation sent. Patient shows up on a day the practice didn't know about. Walk-in conflict. Backed-up waiting room.
Before state C
Both — the hybrid
Phone line and a contact form — two separate inboxes, neither connected to the EHR. Double the channels, double the manual work. Nothing is confirmed until a human intervenes on every single request.

"You didn't lose that patient because of your care. You lost them because you weren't available when they were ready."

Common questions

Things practice owners ask before saying yes.

Every objection below is one we hear regularly. Here's the honest answer to each one.

"I don't want patients dropping themselves into the wrong slots." ↓
Appointment types in the Real-Time Scheduler are mapped directly from your EHR. A patient booking a comprehensive exam can only land in a comprehensive exam slot. A contact lens fitting goes into a contact lens fitting slot. The EHR's own scheduling rules govern what's available — PatientEngage respects them. Practices that want an additional layer of human review can enable manual approval and confirm each request before it's finalized.
"I already have a Book Now button on my site." ↓
If your button sends an email to your front desk, you don't have online booking — you have a contact form. The difference is confirmation. A contact form creates a lead that someone has to chase. The Real-Time Scheduler creates a confirmed patient who received an instant confirmation and is already in your EHR calendar before anyone on your team sees the request. One captures patients. One loses them while you sleep.
"I already pay for Weave or SolutionReach." ↓
Weave is a phone system with scheduling features added. SolutionReach is a recall and retention platform. Neither was designed as a new patient acquisition channel, and neither has Google Reserve integration. The Real-Time Scheduler captures patients at the moment they're searching — in Google, after hours, on mobile — before they ever reach your front desk. It works alongside your existing tools, not instead of them.
"My front desk is already overwhelmed." ↓
That's exactly why this exists. Every appointment the Real-Time Scheduler confirms automatically is one fewer callback, one fewer data entry task, and one fewer "did that get added to the schedule?" conversation. The queue shrinks. The phone stops ringing for bookings. Staff attention shifts to the patients already in the room.
"Our patients prefer to call. We don't want to push them online." ↓
Nobody is pushing your patients anywhere. The Real-Time Scheduler sits alongside your phone line — it doesn't replace it. Patients who prefer to call still call. The Scheduler captures the ones who searched Google at 9pm, the ones who hate being put on hold, the ones who are new to the area and weren't going to call a practice they've never heard of. Those patients weren't going to call anyway. Without the Scheduler, you simply never hear from them.
