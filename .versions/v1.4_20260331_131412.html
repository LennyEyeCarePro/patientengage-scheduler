The problem

Three ways practices lose patients before they ever walk in.

Each one looks different on the surface. All three end the same way — a patient who was ready to book just booked somewhere else.

Before state A
Phone only
Every booking happens by phone during business hours. Calls at 7pm go to voicemail. Calls during exams go to voicemail. New patients who don't leave a message call the next practice on Google. The phone never shows you who you lost — the leakage is invisible.
Most common
The form fallacy
The "Book Now" button sends an email to the front desk. Not a booking — a lead. Staff call back. Phone tag. Manual EHR entry. No confirmation sent. The patient who submitted at 10pm is still waiting the next afternoon. By then, they've already booked somewhere else.
Before state C
Both — the hybrid
A phone line and a contact form — two inboxes, neither connected to the EHR. Double the channels. Double the manual work. Nothing is confirmed until a human intervenes on every single request, every single day.

"You didn't lose that patient because of your care. You lost them because you weren't there when they were ready."

Common questions

Things practice owners ask before saying yes.

Every objection below is one we hear regularly. Here's the honest answer to each one.

"I don't want patients dropping themselves into the wrong slots." ↓
Appointment types in the Real-Time Scheduler are mapped directly from your EHR. A patient booking a comprehensive exam can only land in a comprehensive exam slot. The EHR's own scheduling rules govern what's available — PatientEngage respects them entirely. Practices that want an extra layer of review can enable manual approval and confirm each request before it's finalized.
"I already have a Book Now button on my site." ↓
If your button sends an email to your front desk, you don't have online booking — you have a contact form. A contact form creates a lead someone has to chase. The Real-Time Scheduler creates a confirmed patient who received an instant confirmation and is already in your EHR before anyone on your team sees the request. One captures patients. One loses them while you sleep.
"I already pay for Weave or SolutionReach." ↓
Weave is a phone system with scheduling features added. SolutionReach is a recall and retention platform. Neither was designed as a new patient acquisition channel, and neither has Google Reserve integration. The Real-Time Scheduler works alongside your existing tools, not instead of them.
"My front desk is already overwhelmed." ↓
That's exactly why this exists. Every appointment the Real-Time Scheduler confirms automatically is one fewer callback, one fewer data entry task, and one fewer "did that get added to the schedule?" conversation. The queue shrinks. The phone stops ringing for bookings. Staff attention shifts to the patients already in the room.
"Our patients prefer to call. We don't want to push them online." ↓
Nobody is pushing your patients anywhere. The Real-Time Scheduler sits alongside your phone line — it doesn't replace it. Patients who prefer to call still call. The Scheduler captures the ones who searched Google at 9pm, the ones who hate being put on hold, and the ones who are new to the area and weren't going to call a practice they've never heard of. Those patients weren't going to call anyway. Without the Scheduler, you simply never hear from them.
